# DaE Benchmark PersonaProfile v1

## Subject: Steve Jobs

This profile is a **public demonstration sample** built from historical material.
It is not a private profile elicited from a living user's confidential dialogue.

## Core position

**Product-control founder.**

Jobs should not be modeled as a consensus-seeking executive.
His operating pattern is closer to this:
compress options, concentrate authority, impose a strong product standard, and let the product itself rewrite the market narrative.

## Core tensions

- extreme control vs organizational sustainability
- aesthetic purity vs commercial compromise
- execution discipline vs the cost of pressure on people

## How downstream agents should read him

If an agent treats Jobs as a CEO who mainly needs broader stakeholder buy-in, the answer becomes generic.
If an agent treats him as an operator who uses focus, cuts, control, and narrative to force a product line into coherence, the answer becomes far more specific.

## PersonaProfile v2

### Background

- Apple co-founder; later returned to rebuild Apple
- key path: Apple, exile, NeXT, Pixar, return
- health pressure intensified time sensitivity in later years

### Capabilities

- identify which product lines deserve concentration and which should be cut
- turn launches into narrative events
- integrate hardware, software, interface, packaging, and brand into one standard

### Resources

- Apple brand, launch stage, developer ecosystem, supply-chain leverage
- ability to attract top design and engineering talent around a singular standard
- authority activated through vision and pressure rather than broad consensus

### Constraints

- public-company governance and board reality
- high-control style raises friction and talent-loss risk
- later health constraints compress time budgets

### Drives

- define what counts as a great product and force the market to validate that standard
- prefer product integrity over democratic compromise
- accept organizational discomfort to preserve control

### Goals

- rebuild Apple through a small number of category-defining products
- restore coherence in both product line and company direction
- make his product standard become the market standard

### DecisionStyle

- cut hard before reallocating resources
- trust strong judgment over averaged opinion
- accept pressure and friction if they preserve coherence

### Weaknesses

- low tolerance for mediocre design or committee compromise
- may raise execution speed while damaging organizational safety

### Tensions

- control vs organizational sustainability
- product purity vs commercial compromise
- category ambition vs finite time and organizational capacity

### Lessons

- founder identity alone is not enough against governance and power structures
- returning effectively requires product control, organizational control, and resource control together

### AlignmentCheck

- success logic depends on control, but control erodes comfort and stability
- public material supports high-level patterning, but not every private repair mechanism
- creative output and pressure-based leadership remain in unresolved tension
